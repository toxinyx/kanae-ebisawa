Blame, Understanding, Rationalisation and Sacrifice

Kanae learned very early on into her childhood that the situation her family was in was not really anybody's fault. And so, from a very early age, she learned to rationalise the long work hours of her parents and the chores that would quickly pile up due to their absence. The little quality time that was given to her was quickly compartmentalised as a necessary sacrifice to stay afloat - and so the new norm slowly arose.
